Lessons

Simple Present Tense

Formula: Subject + V1 (s/es) + Object

Example: She reads a book every day.

Usage: Describes habitual actions, general truths, and fixed arrangements. It can also be used for scheduled events in the near future..

Present Continuous Tense

Formula: Subject + am/is/are + V-ing + Object

Example: She is reading a book now.

Usage: Describes actions currently happening at the moment of speaking or temporary actions occurring around the present time. It can also indicate planned future events.

Present Perfect Tense

Formula: Subject + have/has + V3 + Object

Example: She has read the book.

Usage: Describes actions that occurred at an unspecified time in the past but are relevant or connected to the present. It emphasizes the result of the action.

Present Perfect Continuous Tense

Formula: Subject + have/has + been + V-ing + Object

Example: She has been reading the book for two hours.

Usage: Describes actions that began in the past and continue into the present or were happening recently and have a connection to now. It emphasizes the duration of the action.

Simple Past Tense

Formula: Subject + V2 + Object

Example: She read a book yesterday.

Usage: Describes actions that were completed at a specific time in the past. The time can be stated or implied.

Past Continuous Tense

Formula: Subject + was/were + V-ing + Object

Example: She was reading a book at 7 PM last night.

Usage: Describes actions that were in progress at a specific time in the past. It often sets the scene for another action that occurred.

Past Perfect Tense

Formula: Subject + had + V3 + Object

Example: She had read the book before going to bed.

Usage: Describes actions that were completed before another action or time in the past. It highlights the sequence of events.

Past Perfect Continuous Tense

Formula: Subject + had + been + V-ing + Object

Example: She had been reading the book for two hours before going to bed.

Usage: Describes actions that were ongoing over a period of time in the past and were completed before another action or time in the past.

Simple Future Tense

Formula: Subject + will + V1 + Object

Example: She will read a book tomorrow.

Usage: Describes actions that will happen in the future. It often uses "will" or "shall" to indicate future intent or prediction.

Future Continuous Tense

Formula: Subject + will be + V-ing + Object

Example: She will be reading a book at 7 PM tomorrow.

Usage: Describes actions that will be in progress at a specific time in the future. It emphasizes the ongoing nature of the action.

Future Perfect Tense

Formula: Subject + will have + V3 + Object

Example: She will have read the book by tomorrow.

Usage: Describes actions that will be completed before a certain time or another action in the future. It shows the completion of an action.

Future Perfect Continuous Tense

Formula: Subject + will have been + V-ing + Object

Example: She will have been reading the book for two hours by the time you arrive.

Usage: Describes actions that will have been ongoing for a period of time before a specific future time or event. It emphasizes the duration leading up to that point.
